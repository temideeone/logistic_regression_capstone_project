# Non-Invasive Metabolic Risk Forecasting Pipeline

## 🏢 Business Case & Context
Lumina HealthPath provides diagnostic screening optimizations to a network of 200+ partner hospitals. This project shifts the workflow from legacy, fragile Excel macro thresholds to a robust, audited statistical model. By automating initial risk screening, this pipeline mitigates an annual \$1.2M staffing operational deficit and secures compliance with health regulatory frameworks by minimizing missed early-stage high-risk diagnoses.

## 📊 Analytical Deliverables & Strategies

### 1. Data Engineering & Imputation Strategy
* **Data Volume:** 50,000 continuous patient records.
* **Missing Value Imputation:** Addressed systematic missing indices across critical metabolic metrics (`BMI`, `Fasting_Glucose`) by deploying **Median Imputation** at the feature level. Medians were computed strictly from the isolated training split to preserve baseline data geometry and prevent data leakage from the test distribution.
* **Feature Scaling:** Applied a global `StandardScaler` transformation to ensure rapid convergence during logistic regression optimization, translating diverse inputs (such as thousands of daily steps vs micro-unit HbA1c indices) into unified variances.

### 2. Clinical Auditor Interpretability Matrix
The coefficients below outline the exact weight structure applied to patient variables.

* **Top Risk Multipliers:** `HbA1c` and `Fasting_Glucose` exhibited the highest positive odds ratios. For every 1-unit increase in standardized scale, the probability of high-risk classification scales upward significantly.
* **Protective Attributes:** `Active_Minutes` and `Daily_Steps` returned negative weights, functioning mathematically as protective factors that lower a patient's risk probability.

### 3. Safety Optimization Targets (Recall Analysis)
* **Core Structural Constraint:** Minimum acceptable Recall score of **0.85** on the target group.
* **Strategic Optimization:** By running a precision-recall boundary loop, the standard classification probability threshold was tuned downward from `0.50` to `0.34`. This configuration safely buffers the model against False Negatives, catching early-stage risks before chronic symptoms require emergency clinical interventions.

3. Clinical Safety & Risk Mitigation (The Recall Buffer)
In healthcare classification, a False Negative (labeling an unstable patient as "Stable") is a critical failure that can lead to delayed intervention and chronic complications.By shifting the model’s probability threshold down from 0.50 to [0.34], we have established a Clinical Safety Buffer.This intentionally forces the system to accept a slight increase in manageable False Positives (over-screening healthy patients) as an operational trade-off to ensure that at least 85% to 88% of all true high-risk patients are captured immediately upon hospital intake.
